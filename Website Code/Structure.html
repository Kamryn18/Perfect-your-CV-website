How to structure your CV

What is a CV?

Your CV is document used to sell yourself to prospective employers.It should highlight why they should hire you, why you're the best person for the job. Think of it as a mini-advert, starring you.

What to include in your CV

  1. Name, address and contact details: this should be at the top of your CV
    2.
    • Your contact details should include your professional email address and phone number
    • You can also include a link to your LinkedIn profile
  2. Personal Profile (personal statement)
    3.

    This is a short paragraph (just a few lines) at the top of the page, to attract the reader's attention. This should be a short summary of your CV, which includes as many transferable skills, and skills relevant to the role as possible

    Your personal profile should address the following:

    • Who you are
    • You career aims
    • What makes you an ideal/suitbale candidate for the role
  3. Education
    4.

    This should be listed in reverse chronoligcal order (start with you most recent qualifications), and should include:

    • The names of the instituitons you have attended (or are currently attending), and the dates you were there
    • The qualifications and grades you achieved
    • If you have a degree, you can also inculude any relevant modules or projects
  4. Work experience and emplyoment history
    5.

    As with your education, your work experience should be listed in reverse chronological order.

    • Provide a line to summarise the role
    • Relate the skills you learnt, and experiences to the role you are applying for
    • Try to use a consistent number of bullet points e.g. 3 for each experience
      • Tip: Follow the Tripe O rule (what each bullet point should consist of):
        1. Overview: the role and what it involves
        2. Opportunity: what you did for them
        3. what have you achieved (put it in number terms or as a percentage)
    • Try and make ALL your experiences relevant; frame them in a way that makes them relevant
  5. Interests and achievements
    6.

    This should include any extracurricular activites, hobbies, interests, and achievements that are revelant to the job. It is your opporunity to show off a little bit!

    Extra Tips:
  1. Try and make your CV one page long if possible. Your CV should not be more than two pages
  2. Make sure you chose a clear font like Arial or Calibri, and a clear font size
  3. Your format should remian consistent throughout
